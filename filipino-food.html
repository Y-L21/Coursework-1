The Art of Filipino Cuisine

Embark on a journey where you explore a variety of dishes native to the Philippines and the culture behind them.

About

The Philippines is an archipelagic country located in the depths of south-east Asia. It has 7,107 islands which holds the title for the most amount of islands in a given country. The official language is Tagalog as well as approximately 110 other spoken dialects. The Philippines is notorious for it's magnificent landscape which attracts tourist from around the world.

Meals

Adobo

Adobo is an authentic Filipino dish which many tourist love. It is simple to make but sarap (delicious). There are two types of Adobo manok (chicken) and baboy (pork). There are differnt methods to cooking Adobo depending on the region in the Philippines.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a sour soup which Filipino's love on any occassion. You can adjust how sour the soup is so that all can enjoy. The ingredients for Sinigang consist of a variety of vegetables such as string beans, egg plant, spinach, etc. with either pork, chicken or fish

Lumpia

Lumpia also known as Spring rolls is a very popular starter oriental starter. There are two types of Lumpia; fresh Lumpia and the regular fried Lumpia. The fillings can be either vegetable or meat.

Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a stew that has a thick savory peanut sauce with vegetables like pokchoi, egg plant, string beans, banana heart (mainly). It's a mixture of Oxtail and beef.

Desserts

Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a comfort food. It is a popular dessert to have during summer time. In literal transation it stands for 'mix-mix' as it contains a mixture of different sweetened fruits, beans, ice cream, evaporated milk, etc. The ingredients are fully customisable to the persons appetite. Ube ice cream (made from purple yam) is a very popular flavour of ice cream to go with the Halo-Halo.

Leche Flan

Leche Flan is another famous dessert that is eaten in every occassion. It's made of milk, egg yolk and is steamed.

Mango Float

This dessert contains layers of biscuit's, rich cream and ripe Mango. It is a sweet and savoury delicasy that thrives within the Filipino community.

Puto

Puto is a steamed ground rice with sugar and coconut milk. It has a soft and fluffy texture. Puto is usually eaten with hot chocolate or dinuguan.
